What is Child Abuse?

Child abuse is the maltreatment of a child by a parent or guardian who is belittling their child emotionally, mentally, and/or causing bodily harm.

TYPES OF CHILD ABUSE:

  • Physical Abuse:

    • Physical abuse is when a child suffers planned/ non-accidental
    corporal injury at the hands of a parent or caregiver

  • Verbal Abuse:

    • Verbal abuse is any behavior or language which is aimed to
    persuade its victims to doubt thier abilities and capabilites while
    also becoming subdued by the abuser.

  • Neglect:

    • Child neglect occurs when parents, guardians or caregivers
    do not give the appropriate care, affection, supervision and emotional
    support necessary for a healthy upbringing.

  • Sexual Abuse:

    • This form of abuse occurs when an older adolescent or adult
    uses children for sexual gratification. Child sexual abuse can
    occur in a variety of environments such as home, school, or work.

  • Emotional Abuse:

    • Emotional child abuse comes in many forms. Abuse could either
    involve insulting or belittling words or actions to the child,
    or be total indifference that results in emotional insatbility.

  • Domestic Violence:

    • Violence can be criminal and include physical assault like
    hitting, pushing, shoving, unconsensual sexual activity, and stalking.

    WHAT WE DO

    Challenge Child Abuse is trying to end and prevent child abuse. We have created a device called "The Wristband" that will allow
    children to communicate with someone from our headquarters. The wristband has two buttons. When the wristband
    turns on it will ask you how you are feeling today. Then you can respond by pressing button a for a smiley face
    or button b for a frowny face. If you have an emergency you press a then b then both at the same time to send an emergency
    call to headquarters. Finally headquarters will respond by telling you help is on the way.
