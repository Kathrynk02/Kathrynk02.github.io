CHILD ABUSE

What is Child Abuse?

Child abuse is the maltreatment of a child by a parent or guardian who is belittling their child emotionally, mentally, causing bodily harm.

There are many different types of child abuse.

TYPES OF CHILD ABUSE

  1. Physical Abuse:

    2. Physical abuse is when a child suffers planned/ non-accidental corporal injury at the hands of a parent or caregiver

  2. Verbal Abuse:

    3. Verbal abuse is any behavior or language which is aimed to persuade its victims to doubt thier abilities and capabilites while also becoming subdued by the abuser.

  3. Neglect:

    4. Child neglect occurs when parents, guardians or caregivers do not give the appropriate care, affection, supervision and emotional support necessary for a healthy upbringing.

  4. Sexual Abuse:

    5. This form of abuse occurs when an older adolescent or adult uses children for sexual gratification. Child sexual abuse can occur in a variety of environments such as home, school, or work.

  5. Emotional Abuse:

    6. Emotional child abuse comes in many forms abuse could either involve insulting or belittling words or actions to the child, or be total indifference that results in emotional insatbility.

  6. Domestic Violence:

    7. Violence can be criminal and include physical assault like hitting, pushing, shoving, unconsensual sexual activity, and stalking.
